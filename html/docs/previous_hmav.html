Footnotes:


C. elegans: For western analysis, nuclear extracts were prepared from N2 embryos and recombinant histone H3 was purchased from Active motif, cat #31207. A 3-fold dilution series of both total nuclear extract and recombinant histone H3 was loaded on a 12.5% SDS gel, using recombinant histone H3 levels that were comparable to H3 in the nuclear extract. For an antibody to pass, the histone H3 band in the nuclear extract constituted at least 50% of the nuclear protein signal, showed at least 10-fold enrichment relative to any other single band, and showed at least 10-fold enriched signal relative to unmodified histone.


C. elegans: Most ChIP experiments were performed on two stages: early embryos were collected from young gravid wild-type hermaphrodites and fixed with formaldehyde, or L3 larvae were frozen, ground into powder, and fixed in formaldehyde. Chromatin was prepared and sheared, and ChIP was performed using antibodies to histone marks. “Consistent” means that ChIP patterns were reproducible among replicates and make biological sense. For example, ChIP-chip patterns for H3K4me3, H3K36me3, and H3K9me3 were generally as expected from work in other systems: H3K4me3 enriched at the 5' end of actively expressed genes, H3K36me3 enriched over the body of actively expressed genes, H3K9me3 enriched over silent genes. “Consistent*” were consistent in L3 larval extracts, but not in early embryo extracts.
